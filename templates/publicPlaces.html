{% if placeType.name == 'Restaurants': %}

Perhaps you're wondering why Nuevo México only lists New Mexican food? It's because if you're in New Mexico, you really should eat New Mexican food. You can't get it anywhere else, and much more importantly, it's some of the best food in the world. (If you're really intent on eating food like pizza, at least try it with green chile on top. Or try a hamburger with thick slices of roasted green chile.) When you order New Mexican food, you'll be asked if you would like red or green chile with your dish. Both are very good. And if you can't decide which one you want, ask for Christmas (both red and green).

{% elif placeType.name == 'Pueblos': %}

There are 19 pueblos in New Mexico and three reservations (with the Navajo Nation being the largest). See here for a full list. We've included five of the pueblos below. Please note, however, that not all pueblos allow visitors at any time and many have restrictions on photography and video use. Make sure to always check visitor information before visiting a pueblo.

{% elif placeType.name == 'Museums': %}

There are lots of great museums in New Mexico. We've only included a small sampling, but you can see Wikipedia for a good list.

{% elif placeType.name == 'Shopping spots': %}

Nuevo México focuses primarily on shopping spots where you can buy items that are typically not sold outside of the state. We try to help you find great places to buy New Mexico-specific jewelry, crafts, pottery, art, baskets, collectibles, etc. The state's 19 pueblos are some of the best places to buy items like this; please note, however, that that not all pueblos allow visitors at any time and many have restrictions on photography and video use. Make sure to always check visitor information before visiting a pueblo.
